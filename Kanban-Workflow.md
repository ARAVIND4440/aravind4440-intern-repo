# Agile Workflows & Kanban

## How does a Kanban board work, and how does it help manage workflow?

A Kanban board is a visual workflow tool used to manage tasks. Tasks are shown as cards and moved across columns to represent progress.

## What do the different columns on a Kanban board represent? (e.g., Backlog, In Progress, Blocked, Done)

- Not Started : Tasks not yet started
- In Progress: Tasks currently being worked on
- Ready for review: Tasks completed and waiting for review
- Approved by Bot: Tasks validated by automated checks
- Done: Completed tasks

## How do tasks move through the board, and who is responsible for updating them?

Tasks move from left to right based on their progress. The person working on the task is responsible for updating and moving it, he is named as scrum master .

## What are the benefits of limiting work in progress (WIP)?

Limiting work in progress helps reduce overload, improves focus, and ensures tasks are completed faster.

## Reflection

### How does Kanban help manage priorities and avoid overload?

Kanban helps by making tasks visible and ensuring important tasks are handled first. It avoids overload by limiting how many tasks are worked on at the same time.

### How can you improve your workflow using Kanban principles?

I can improve my workflow by focusing on fewer tasks, updating task status regularly, and identifying blocked tasks early.
