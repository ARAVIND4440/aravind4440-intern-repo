# Help Strategy Reflection

## When do you prefer using AI vs. searching Google?

I prefer using Google for simple and direct problems, such as error messages, syntax issues, or looking up documentation. It is quick and gives precise answers.

I prefer using AI tools like ChatGPT when I need a deeper understanding of a concept, want code explanations, or need help thinking through a problem. AI is especially useful when I am not sure how to approach a problem or need step-by-step guidance.

## How do you decide when to ask a colleague instead?

I would ask a colleague when:
- The problem involves sensitive or company-specific information  
- I have been stuck on a problem for a long time  
- The issue is complex and requires domain knowledge  
- The task is urgent and needs a quick resolution  

Colleagues can provide context that tools like Google or AI may not have.

## What challenges do developers face when troubleshooting alone?

One of the main challenges is spending too much time stuck on the same problem. Developers may go in the wrong direction without realising it.

Another challenge is misunderstanding the root cause of the issue, especially when dealing with complex systems. This can lead to frustration and reduced productivity.

It can also be difficult to decide when to stop trying alone and seek help, which is an important skill in software development.

## Reflection

From this task, I learned that different resources are useful in different situations. Google is efficient for quick lookups, AI is helpful for understanding and problem-solving, and colleagues are important for context and complex issues.

Using the right resource at the right time can improve productivity and reduce time spent debugging.
